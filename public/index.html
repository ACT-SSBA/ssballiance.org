Section 174 threatens any company that builds software.

You used to be able to deduct the cost of building and maintaining software...until now.

You may have heard about this from your tax preparer recently. They’ve probably told you that your taxes due for 2022 and estimated tax payments for 2023 are way higher than you expected.

That’s because, due to Section 174, any kind of software development is being treated as research and experimental work that must be spread out over many years (“capitalized”). Instead of expensing your, your employees, or your contractors's salaries, you have to spread them out over 5 to 15 years.

That means if you paid someone—yourself, even—say, $100,000 last year to develop and maintain software, only $20,000 of that can be taken as an expense each year, and spread over five years. This means your profit—the amount the IRS can tax you on—just increased by a phantom $80,000.

(It’s even worse if you have developers who live abroad, or live abroad yourself. Costs have to be spread out over 15 years for developers in foreign countries.

Frequently asked questions

Why did this become law in the first place?

When Congress changed the tax laws in 2017, this provision was included. But Congress never intended for this change to become law, and passed bills repealing it last year. But because of squabbles over unrelated issues, they never became law. So Section 174 still stands.

When did Section 174 take effect?

Section 174 took effect for tax year 2022. That means you'll see the impact on your 2022 taxes.

If Section 174 is repealed, will it be retroactive?

Yes, that's the intent.

Why should we expect Congress will listen to us?

Big Tech spent tons of energy and money trying to get this fixed last year. But let’s be honest—Big Tech is not exactly popular on Capitol Hill. When people picture companies that do software development, they picture big companies in Silicon Valley whose founders fly around on private jets, not tiny companies with less than five people in Ohio or. But small businesses are much more sympathetic.
