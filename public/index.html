Sign the National Association of Manufacturers Letter

Deadline: October 23

The National Association of Manufacturers is leading a new letter to Congress regarding Section 174 (R&D/software expensing) and is asking companies from all industries—not just manufacturing—to sign. This is a major push and we need to get as many companies as possible behind this.

The letter the Small Software Business Alliance sent to Congress in April got almost 600 signatures, and we can get at least as many this time. This is not just limited to software companies, so please share far and wide.

People with US companies can sign this letter, including non-citizens. Including non-software companies. If your company is HQ in the US, you can sign (use your US incorporation city/state).

NOTE: If you're outside the US, you need to VPN into the US to sign.

The Small Software Business Alliance is an informal group of small software business founders and other community members who are united to fix Section 174. Software development and other research and development must be expensable.

On April 18th, 2023, we sent a letter to Congress urging them to take action and make software development expensable again. That letter was signed by 594 small software businesses in all 50 states.

Our letter was mentioned in Congressional hearings and by CNBC, Bloomberg, Politico, Stratechery, and more.
